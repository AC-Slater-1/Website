How We Got Here

SaaS promised freedom. You got lock-in.

The cloud was supposed to liberate businesses from expensive, on-premise software. Instead, it created a new kind of rent. Monthly subscriptions that compound year after year, locking your data inside walled gardens, making switching costs so high that canceling feels impossible. The average small business now spends thousands per year on tools that do what a spreadsheet did in 2010 — just with a nicer interface and a recurring charge.

The moat was data. AI dissolved it.

For a decade, these companies survived because they owned proprietary datasets and algorithms. Semrush built $376M in revenue by repackaging Google's own data. Grammarly reached $700M ARR for grammar checking — something GPT now does for free. The moats were never that deep. They just seemed deep before large language models showed up and commoditized the entire intelligence layer overnight.

93 competitors. Every weakness mapped.

We didn't guess. We studied every SaaS product in every category we plan to enter. Documented their features, analyzed their pricing, mapped their technical architectures, and identified exactly where they're vulnerable. The result: a competitive intelligence database covering 22 product categories and 93 individual tools — and a blueprint to replace every one of them.
